Tiaki Moana What is it? Why does it matters? What can you do?

Reduce Plastic Use and Pollution: One of the most impactful actions individuals can take to support ocean conservation in New Zealand is to reduce plastic consumption and minimize plastic waste. This includes using reusable bags, bottles, and containers, avoiding single-use plastics, and participating in beach clean-up initiatives. By reducing plastic pollution, we can help prevent marine debris from harming marine life and ecosystems, as well as mitigate the impacts of plastic on ocean health and biodiversity.

Support Sustainable Seafood Practices: Another important way to contribute to ocean conservation in New Zealand is by making informed choices about seafood consumption. Supporting sustainable fishing practices and choosing seafood that is sourced responsibly helps protect marine ecosystems and supports the long-term viability of fisheries. By opting for sustainably harvested seafood and supporting fisheries that prioritize environmental stewardship, individuals can play a crucial role in ensuring the health and sustainability of New Zealand's marine resources.

ccessing Resources from WWF and Other Organizations: In New Zealand, individuals passionate about ocean conservation can access a wealth of resources and support from organizations like the World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF). WWF offers valuable educational materials, guides, and campaigns focused on marine conservation, including initiatives specific to New Zealand's unique marine environment. From learning about endangered species like the Hector's dolphin to discovering ways to reduce your carbon footprint and support marine protected areas, WWF provides resources and tools to empower individuals to take action and make a positive difference for the oceans. By leveraging these resources and joining forces with organizations like WWF, individuals can amplify their impact and contribute to the collective effort to safeguard New Zealand's oceans for future generations.
